Our Popular Desserts

Cake

  1. Flour – the main structure-building ingredient.
  2. Sugar – for sweetness and moisture retention.
  3. Eggs – for binding, moisture, and structure.
  4. Butter – adds richness, flavor, and a tender crumb.

Gulab Jamun gets its name from "Gulab" (rose, for the rose-scented syrup) and "Jamun" (an Indian berry it resembles in shape and color).

Gulabjam

  1. Khoya (Mawa) – reduced milk solids, the base of traditional Gulab Jamun dough.
  2. All-Purpose Flour (Maida) – used to bind the dough and give structure.
  3. Sugar – used to make the sweet syrup in which the balls are soaked.
  4. Cardamom (Elaichi) – adds fragrance and flavor to the syrup.

Icecream

  1. Milk – forms the base and provides creaminess.
  2. Cream – adds richness and smooth texture.
  3. Sugar – sweetens the ice cream and affects texture.
  4. Vanilla – a common flavoring; also enhances other flavors.

The first written record of ice cream in America was in 1744, and George Washington reportedly spent $200 on ice cream in one summer!

Kalakand

  1. Milk – traditionally reduced to make the base.
  2. Paneer – gives Kalakand its grainy texture.
  3. Sugar – sweetens the dessert.
  4. Cardamom (Elaichi) – adds aroma and flavor.

Kalakand was accidentally invented in Alwar, Rajasthan when a chef mixed fresh paneer into sweetened milk, creating this delicious grainy treat.
